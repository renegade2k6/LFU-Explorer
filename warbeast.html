Warbeast System

üí° What Are Warbeasts?

Warbeasts are powerful mechanical creatures that represent endgame progression in Last Fortress: Underground. Each warbeast features unique visual designs, extensive upgrade paths including stars, remodeling, and surpass nodes, plus an impressive collection of coatings with stat bonuses. With 6 distinct warbeasts ranging from legendary dragons to mythic pandas, this system offers some of the highest stat ceilings in the game!

Warbeast Roster

There are 6 total warbeasts, divided into two quality tiers. Quality 4 warbeasts are easier to access, while Quality 5 warbeasts offer higher caps and premium positioning.

‚ö†Ô∏è Long-Term Investment

Warbeasts are designed as a very long-term progression system. Fully maxing a single warbeast requires months of dedicated resource farming. Plan your investments wisely!

1Ô∏è‚É£ Base Leveling (Levels 1-20)

Each warbeast has a 20-level progression track. Think of this as the foundation - every warbeast must complete this journey first!

üìä How Leveling Works

  • Progression Items: Each warbeast uses specific items (e.g., Dragon Fragments, Hunter Cores)
  • Cumulative Effects: Level 10 grants ALL bonuses from levels 1-10, not just level 10
  • Power Scaling: Each level adds 1,000 power (Level 20 = 20,000 total base power)
  • Stat Diversity: Different levels unlock different stat types (HP, Attack, Defense, Critical, etc.)

Example: Metallic Dragon Level Progression

Level Cost (Items) New Effect Power Gain
1 100 +2% Accuracy +1,000
5 300 +3% Defense +1,000
10 750 +5% HP +1,000

20 1,850 +10% Attack +1,000

üí° Pro Tip: Costs are relatively low for levels 1-10, then spike significantly. Save resources for the final push to level 20!

3Ô∏è‚É£ Remodel/Awakening (36 Stages)

The Remodel system is a long-term endgame grind with 36 stages spanning levels 6-108. Each stage provides cumulative multipliers to your warbeast's stats.

üîÑ How Remodeling Works

  • 36 Sequential Stages: Must complete in order (can't skip)
  • Multiplicative Bonuses: Each stage adds 2-5% to ALL base stats
  • Cumulative Power: Stage 36 = sum of all previous 35 stages!
  • Dragon Coin Sink: Primary currency required (very expensive at higher stages)
Stage Range Level Range Typical Bonus Per Stage Total by End
Stages 1-12 Levels 6-30 +2% per stage ~+24% cumulative
Stages 13-24 Levels 36-66 +3% per stage ~+60% cumulative
Stages 25-36 Levels 72-108 +4-5% per stage ~+120% cumulative

‚ö†Ô∏è Remodel Reality Check

Completing all 36 stages can take 6-12 months of dedicated resource gathering! This is intentionally designed as the ultimate long-term progression goal. Budget your Dragon Coins wisely!

4Ô∏è‚É£ Module System (6 Slots)

Warbeasts have their own dedicated module system with 6 equipment slots. Unlike APCs (which use a refitting system), warbeast modules are specifically designed for these combat units and cannot be shared with other vehicle types.

‚öôÔ∏è Module Types

6 Module Types: Weapon ‚Ä¢ Energy ‚Ä¢ Armor ‚Ä¢ Power ‚Ä¢ Command ‚Ä¢ Core

Module Levels: Level 1 ‚Üí Level 6 (each level provides stronger stat bonuses)

How to Get: Modules are obtained from warbeast activities and rewards, then fused to higher levels

üîÑ Module Fusion (3 Types)

Fusion Type Success Rate Result
Standard Fusion 100% Two same-level modules ‚Üí One module +1 level
Overclocking Fusion Variable Two modules (< Lv. 13) ‚Üí Result: -6 to +6 levels, or damage
Mutation Fusion Variable Single module ‚Üí Up to 8 mutations (upgrade/downgrade/bonus module)

Module Proficiency (Levels 0-10):

  • Proficiency reduces crafting time, not stats
  • Level 0 = 0% time reduction ‚Ä¢ Level 10 = 100% time reduction (instant crafting)
  • Gain proficiency experience through module crafting

üí° Module Upgrade Strategy

  • Early Game: Use Standard Fusion for guaranteed progression
  • Mid Game: Try Overclocking Fusion when you have surplus modules
  • Late Game: Use Mutation Fusion for high-risk, high-reward progression to Level 6
  • Proficiency: Max proficiency = instant module crafting (huge time saver!)

5Ô∏è‚É£ Coating System (37+ Coatings)

Warbeast coatings provide both visual customization AND stat bonuses, making them essential for maximizing combat effectiveness!

‚ö° How Coating Bonuses Work

Each coating provides two types of bonuses:

  • Owning Attribute: Passive bonus granted just for owning the coating (even if not equipped)
  • Equipping Attribute: Additional active bonus when the coating is equipped on your warbeast

Example: Metallic Dragon | Sandy Coating

Bonus Type Stats Granted
Owning Attribute TAC DEF +1% ‚Ä¢ HP +1%
Equipping Attribute WPN DEF +1% ‚Ä¢ TAC DEF +1% ‚Ä¢ HP +1%

üí° Coating Strategy

  • Collect All: Even unequipped coatings provide passive bonuses - collect everything!
  • Quality 5 Coatings: Higher quality coatings typically provide better stat bonuses
  • Stack Bonuses: Owning + Equipping bonuses stack, so equip your best coating on your main warbeast
  • Event Exclusive: Many premium coatings are only available during special events
